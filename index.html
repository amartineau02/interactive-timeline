The home of the pilgrims and Wompanoag is also home to many, many haunting and eerie stories. Some of these stories have a history dating back to the early 1600s. Massachusetts Historic Hauntings is where you can find 12 of the most interesting, most historical ghost stories of the Bay State.

1643

The Rehoboth Village Cemetery

It’s 2019. This year, the town of Rehoboth, MA has turned 375 years old. You’ve spent the day on Route 44, watching the parade and celebrating the history of the small farm town. Once night falls, you pack your things and head home for the day, turning left onto Bay State Road. Going up the hill, you near one of the oldest living landmarks of the town -- the Olde Rehoboth Village Cemetery. As you drive by, you hear a scream, followed by echoing laughter. A man stands in the cemetery, his eye sockets dark and empty.

This particular phantom has been wandering the cemeteries for ages, even into the modern days. His name is unknown, but he appears to be wearing eighteenth-century clothing and is primarily found in the southwestern corner of the grounds. It’s been recorded that these sightings can range from seeing his apparition on the horizon in front of a stone to him chasing after a car and banging on the car windows. He targets women especially, and sometimes he’s apparition has been seen beating a woman. (It is believed he may have bludgeoned a woman to death in his lifetime.) Along with this, do not ever, ever say “Catherine”. He yells this name with a particular hatred and spews vulgarities with it.
